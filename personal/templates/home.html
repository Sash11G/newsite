THE MATHEMATICAL UNIVERSE

The Algebraic Mirror

In a Cartesian cosmos, you occupy the origin—every vector, metric tensor, and coordinate system is defined relative to your point of view.

Physical laws arise from symmetry groups (SO(3), U(1)), whose generators encode conservation via Noether’s theorem. Fractal scaling laws—power laws and zeta functions—bridge quantum fluctuations and galactic structures.

Each measurement collapses a wavefunction into an eigenstate, folding the observer into the formalism. Here, self and universe are algebraically isomorphic: the “equation” that describes your consciousness is the same that governs the fabric of reality.

Information theory enters as Shannon entropy—every bit exchanged between you and the cosmos reshapes the state space, compressing infinite possibilities into finite observations. The act of observation is itself a map from a Hilbert space to a discrete datum.
